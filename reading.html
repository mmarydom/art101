Q2. From the Manovich reading, what is the difference between visualization and mapping?
Manovich explained that visualization and mapping are two closely related subjects - in fact, he states that visualization can be considered as a subset of mapping. However, in his article, he states that visualization is processing data even in terms of something that is not visual, while mapping is the process of mapping one representation into another.

Q3. When Manovich states that visualization is the anti-sublime art what is he referring to? Do you agree with this notion?
Manovich refers to when visualization artists try map data into something similar to human perception and cognition. In my opinion, I think that visualization and mapping can be both anti-sublime and sublime, as long as it is within that artistic realm. If you want to use these technologcal methods as a means for representation beyond human comprehension or something grandeour, as an artist you have the power to do so.
